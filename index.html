What is GWA in the Philippines?

General Weighted Average (GWA) is the academic average used by many Philippine educational institutions. It considers both the grade received and the number of units per subject. This makes GWA a more accurate measure of academic performance compared to a simple average of grades.

How to Compute GWA

The standard formula for computing GWA is: (Grade × Units) ÷ Total Units. Each subject grade is multiplied by its corresponding units. The sum of all weighted grades is then divided by the total number of units taken during the semester.

Manual Example of GWA Calculation

Suppose a student has the following grades:
Math: 1.75 (3 units)
English: 2.00 (3 units)
Science: 1.50 (4 units)

Multiply each grade by units, add them, and divide by total units:
(1.75×3) + (2.00×3) + (1.50×4) = 5.25 + 6 + 6 = 17.25
Total Units = 10
GWA = 17.25 ÷ 10 = 1.73

Difference Between GWA and GPA

GWA and GPA both measure academic performance but may differ in grading scale and computation method. GWA commonly follows the Philippine grading system, while GPA is often used in international or American-based systems. Some schools may convert GWA to GPA for scholarship or exchange program requirements.

Common Grading Scale in Philippine Universities

Many universities use a grading scale where:
1.00 – Excellent
1.25 to 1.75 – Very Good
2.00 to 2.75 – Good / Satisfactory
3.00 – Passing
5.00 – Failed

Why GWA is Important for Students

Students monitor their GWA to evaluate academic progress every term. It plays a major role in determining eligibility for Latin honors, scholarship qualifications, dean’s list recognition, and retention in degree programs. Maintaining a strong GWA helps students stay competitive academically.

GWA and Latin Honors Eligibility

Latin honors such as Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude, and Summa Cum Laude are usually based on a student’s final GWA. Each university sets its own required GWA threshold, which students must meet along with other academic policies.

Scholarship and Academic Requirements

Many scholarships require students to maintain a specific GWA every semester. Failing to meet the required average may lead to probation or loss of financial assistance. That is why regularly computing GWA helps students stay aware of their academic standing.

Tips to Maintain a Good GWA

Students can maintain a strong GWA by monitoring grades each term, focusing on high-unit subjects, and improving performance in difficult courses. Consistent study habits and early grade tracking help prevent academic risks and support long-term academic goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is GWA different from GPA?

Yes, both measure academic performance but may follow different grading scales depending on the institution.

How often should I compute my GWA?

It is recommended to compute your GWA every semester to track academic progress and eligibility for honors.

Do all universities use the same grading system?

No, grading systems may vary slightly across universities, so students should always check their school’s official grading policy.
